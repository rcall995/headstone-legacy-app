What is Cemetery Scouting?

Cemetery Scouts are volunteers who visit cemeteries and document grave locations using their smartphone's GPS. This creates a searchable database that helps families find their loved ones.

Unlike other genealogy sites that rely on user submissions, our Scout program actively fills in the gaps. When you scout, you're not just adding data—you're helping reunite families with ancestors they've been searching for.

How Scouting Works

1

Visit a Cemetery

Head to any cemetery—local or while traveling. Every grave documented helps.

2

Stand at the Grave

Position yourself at the headstone. Your phone's GPS captures the exact location.

3

Drop a Pin

Tap to drop a GPS pin. Add the name from the headstone and optionally snap a photo.

4

Help Families

Your pin becomes searchable. Families can now find and visit their ancestor's grave.
